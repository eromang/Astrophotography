## Stellarium Cross-Check

Refreshed 2026-05-22 10:24 CEST via Stellarium 1.x Remote Control API. **Root cause of yesterday's "altitude bug" identified and fixed:** setting `time=` while `timerate≠0` doesn't freeze the simulation — Stellarium immediately advances the clock back from the set value. **Workaround:** send `timerate=0` alongside `time=` to lock the clock. With that fix the API now agrees with internal calc.

Queried at JD 2461183.458333 = 01:00 CEST May 23 (M16 window opens, alt rising past balcony SE building floor):

| Target | Internal alt | Stellarium alt | Δ alt | Internal az | Stellarium az | Δ az | HA | Status |
|--------|-------------|----------------|-------|-------------|---------------|------|----|----|
| M16 | 15.97° | 15.98° | 0.01° | 134° | 136.13° | 2.13° | -2h53m | ✓ |

Both values agree well within the ±1° tolerance (the ~2° azimuth offset is from Stellarium using its preset observer location 49.7498°N 6.166°E vs the balcony preset 49.71731°N 6.00823°E — a few km east).

**Secondary API quirk noted:** alt/az values appear to be **cached at the moment of focus** and don't refresh on subsequent time changes, even though `hourAngle` does. To re-cross-check at a different time, the sequence must be: `time= + timerate=0` → deselect → focus → query. The HA, rise, transit, and set values are always live and trustworthy.
