## Rotator Calibration (do once at start of campaign)

The William Optics rotator scale on the RedCat is **mechanical** — its "0°" mark is arbitrary, not aligned to sky north. Calibrate once on Apr 20, then reuse the offset for all 4 nights:

1. **Slew to Mel 111** and let mount settle
2. **Take a 10 s test frame** at gain 100, current rotator position (e.g., 0° on the scale)
3. **Plate-solve** in ASIAIR (Settings → Plate Solving → Solve current image) → reads back the **Position Angle (PA)** in degrees east of north
4. **Compute offset**: `offset = scale_position − sky_PA`. Example: rotator at scale 0°, plate-solve says PA 47° → offset = -47°. To get sky PA = 0° (north up), rotate to scale `0° + 47° = 47°`
5. **Adjust rotator** to the computed scale position, take another 10 s frame, re-solve to verify PA ≈ 0°
6. **Lock the rotator clamp** so it doesn't shift during the meridian flip
7. **Record the calibrated offset** in this note's notes section for reference on Apr 21–23

Once calibrated, the rotator stays locked across all 4 nights — no recalibration needed unless the optical train is disturbed. Same offset applies because the OTA sits in the same saddle position.
