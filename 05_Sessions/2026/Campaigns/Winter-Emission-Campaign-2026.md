# Winter Emission Nebulae — Late 2026 Campaign

Build deep integration on the classic winter emission targets. All Quad Band, all rising late night from October, becoming prime in November–December.

---

## Campaign Priority: HIGH

Several targets with little or no ASI2600MC data. M42 has ~6h but benefits from more. Rosette has only ~1.7h. NGC 2264, IC 443, and NGC 1499 have **zero ASI2600MC data**.

---

> [!info] Night tiling strategy
> These targets rise in sequence through the winter night. A typical December session can tile 2–3 targets: NGC 1499 (early, brief window) or IC 443 (early), then NGC 2264, then M42/Rosette (peaking around midnight–2:00).

## Target Priority Per Night

When choosing which target to shoot on a given night:

```
Is M42 above 25°?
├── YES, and < 15h total → Shoot M42
├── YES, but > 15h total → Shoot Rosette or NGC 2264
└── NO (too early) →
    ├── IC 443 above 30°? → Shoot IC 443
    ├── NGC 1499 in south window? → Grab 1.5h on California
    └── Wait for M42/Rosette to rise
```
