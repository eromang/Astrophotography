## Backfocus Configuration

The correct backfocus distance (59.7mm from M48 thread) is critical for flat field performance across the image circle.

![RedCat 51 Backfocus Diagram](/01_Equipment/Manuals/RedCat-51/WILLIAM%20OPTICS.webp.jpeg)

### Backfocus Stack

| Component | Distance |
|-----------|----------|
| M48 to sensor (total) | 59.7mm |
| Adjust with spacer rings to reach correct distance | — |

> **Tip:** Incorrect backfocus causes star elongation in the corners (tilt-like effect). Measure the backfocus chain carefully: adapter + filter thickness + camera sensor distance must equal 59.7mm.

## Characteristics

### Strengths

- **Fast f/4.9** — captures faint nebulae efficiently, short exposures needed
- **Wide field** — 5.4° x 3.6° frames large nebulae (North America, Orion, Rosette)
- **Flat field** — Petzval design delivers sharp stars corner-to-corner
- **Lightweight** — 1.76 kg fully loaded, well within [[iOptron-CEM26]] capacity (12 kg)
- **No coma/field curvature** — no flattener needed (Petzval is inherently flat)

### Limitations

- **Manual helical focus** — no Crayford/rack-and-pinion; the [[ZWO-EAF]] compensates
- **Short focal length** — small galaxies and planetary nebulae appear tiny at 250mm
- **Fixed focal length** — no focal reducer/extender options
- **Dew-prone** — short dew shield; a dew heater strip is recommended
