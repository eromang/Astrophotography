# Signal-to-Noise Ratio (SNR)

SNR measures how much useful signal (photons from the target) stands above the noise floor. Higher SNR means more detail can be extracted during processing.

---

## The SNR Equation

For a single sub-exposure:

```
                        S
SNR_single = ─────────────────────────
             sqrt(S + B + D*t + R^2)
```

| Symbol | Meaning | Unit |
|--------|---------|------|
| **S** | Signal from the target (photon count) | e- |
| **B** | Sky background (light pollution + airglow) | e- |
| **D** | Dark current (thermal noise rate) | e-/s |
| **t** | Exposure time | s |
| **R** | Read noise (single readout) | e- |

For N stacked subs:

```
SNR_total = SNR_single * sqrt(N)
```

This is why doubling integration time (2x more subs) improves SNR by only ~41% (sqrt(2) = 1.41). Quadrupling integration doubles SNR.

---

## Noise Sources

### Photon Noise (Shot Noise)

Inherent statistical variation in photon arrival. Scales as sqrt(S). This is the dominant noise source in well-exposed deep sky images.

- **Cannot be eliminated** — fundamental to the physics of light
- Reduced by collecting more photons (longer integration)

### Sky Background Noise

Photon noise from the sky itself (light pollution, airglow, moonlight). Scales as sqrt(B).

- Worse at low altitude (more atmosphere = more scatter)
- Worse near the moon or horizon
- **Narrowband filters** (like [[Antlia-FQuad]]) dramatically reduce B by rejecting broadband sky glow

### Dark Current

Thermal electrons generated by the sensor. Scales with temperature and exposure time.

- [[ASI2600MCPro]] at -10C: ~0.0012 e-/s/pixel — negligible
- [[ASI2600MCPro]] at -20C: even lower
- **Effectively zero** for cooled CMOS at these temperatures — not a limiting factor

### Read Noise

Electronic noise from each sensor readout. Fixed per frame regardless of exposure.

- [[ASI2600MCPro]] at Gain 100: ~1.5 e-
- Read noise matters most for **short exposures** where signal is low
- At 300s exposures with f/4.9, sky background dominates over read noise (sky-limited)

## Factors Affecting SNR

### Altitude and Airmass

Airmass measures how much atmosphere light passes through. At zenith (90deg) airmass = 1. At lower altitudes:

```
Airmass = 1 / sin(altitude)
```

| Altitude | Airmass | Signal Loss | Notes |
|----------|---------|-------------|-------|
| 90deg | 1.00 | 0% | Zenith — best possible |
| 60deg | 1.15 | ~10% | Excellent |
| 45deg | 1.41 | ~20% | Good |
| 30deg | 2.00 | ~35% | Acceptable |
| 27deg | 2.20 | ~40% | M16/M17 max from Luxembourg |
| 20deg | 2.92 | ~55% | Poor — avoid if possible |
| 10deg | 5.76 | ~80% | Unusable for deep sky |

Signal loss assumes ~0.25 mag extinction per airmass in narrowband. Broadband extinction is higher (~0.3–0.5 mag/airmass).

At low altitude, sky background also increases (more light pollution scatter through thicker atmosphere), compounding the SNR penalty.

### Exposure Time

Longer subs collect more signal per frame. The goal is to be **sky-limited** — where sky background noise dominates over read noise. At that point, longer individual subs give diminishing returns; total integration time matters more.

With the [[RedCat-51]] at f/4.9 + [[Antlia-FQuad]]:
- **300s** is sky-limited from Bortle 4 — optimal sub length
- Going shorter (e.g., 120s) increases read noise contribution
- Going longer risks tracking issues and satellite/plane losses

### Filter Choice

| Filter | Sky Background Rejection | Best For |
|--------|--------------------------|----------|
| [[Antlia-FQuad]] (narrowband) | ~90% of broadband sky rejected | Emission nebulae, moonlit nights |
| [[Optolong-LPro]] (broadband) | ~30–50% LP rejection | Galaxies, clusters, reflection nebulae |
| No filter | No rejection | Unusable from Bortle 4+ |

Narrowband filters are the single biggest SNR advantage for emission nebulae from light-polluted or moonlit skies.

### Astronomical Darkness

True astronomical darkness requires the sun below -18deg. Without it, the sky background (B) is elevated.

| Darkness Level | Sun Below Horizon | Sky Impact |
|----------------|-------------------|------------|
| Astronomical twilight | < -18deg | True dark sky |
| Nautical twilight | -12deg to -18deg | Sky 2-5x brighter |
| Civil twilight | -6deg to -12deg | Sky 10-50x brighter |

At latitude 49.6deg (Tuntange), astronomical darkness is:
- **Absent** from ~June 5 to ~July 8 (sun never reaches -18deg)
- **Brief** (< 3h) in late May and mid-July
- **Full** (> 8h) from September through March

This directly impacts SNR for summer targets.

### Moon

| Moon Phase | Illumination | Narrowband Impact | Broadband Impact |
|------------|-------------|-------------------|------------------|
| New | 0% | None | None |
| Crescent | < 25% | Negligible | Minor if > 30deg away |
| Quarter | ~50% | Minor | Significant — +50% sky background |
| Gibbous | 75%+ | Moderate | Severe — 2-3x sky background |
| Full | 100% | Noticeable | Unusable for faint targets |

The [[Antlia-FQuad]] makes moonlit nights viable for emission nebulae — one of its key advantages.

## SNR Quality Benchmarks

Target SNR on the **nebula or extended object** (not stars):

| SNR | Quality | What You See |
|-----|---------|--------------|
| < 5 | Detection limit | Object barely visible above noise |
| 5–10 | Marginal | Main structure hints, very noisy |
| 10–20 | Usable | Core structure visible, noisy background |
| 20–30 | Good | Fine detail clear, smooth enough for processing |
| **25–30** | **Session planning target** | **Minimum for publishable results** |
| 30–50 | Very good | Aggressive stretching holds up, faint extensions visible |
| 50+ | Excellent | Deep faint detail, smooth gradients |

For **HDR targets** (M42 Trapezium, M16 Pillars, M17 bar):
- Bright core: SNR 50+ easily reached
- Faint outer regions: SNR 25+ needed — drives total integration time

## Integration Time Estimates

Approximate total integration needed to reach SNR 25 on nebula emission, using [[RedCat-51]] + [[ASI2600MCPro]] + [[Antlia-FQuad]] at Gain 100, 300s subs:

| Scenario | Altitude | Darkness | Est. Integration |
|----------|----------|----------|------------------|
| Ideal (winter target, high) | > 50deg | Full astro dark | 3–5h |
| Good (autumn/spring) | 35–50deg | Full astro dark | 5–8h |
| Moderate (summer, well-placed) | > 50deg | Short dark window | 8–12h |
| Challenging (low altitude) | 25–30deg | Limited darkness | 12–20h |
| Extreme (low + no astro dark) | < 30deg | No astro darkness | 20–30h+ |

These are rough guides. Actual SNR depends on target surface brightness, exact sky conditions, and seeing.

## Multi-Night Accumulation

For targets requiring many hours, stack data across multiple nights or months:

```
SNR_total = SNR_single_sub * sqrt(N_total_subs)
```

| Nights | Integration (4h/night) | SNR vs 1 Night |
|--------|------------------------|----------------|
| 1 | 4h | 1.0x |
| 2 | 8h | 1.4x |
| 4 | 16h | 2.0x |
| 9 | 36h | 3.0x |
| 16 | 64h | 4.0x |

**Requirements for cross-session stacking:**
- Same gain, temperature, and filter across all sessions
- Flats per session (dust and rotation change)
- Darks from [[Master-Library]] at matching exposure/temperature
- Use **SubframeSelector** to reject poor subs (high FWHM, eccentricity, sky background outliers)
- **ImageIntegration** with winsorized sigma clipping handles varying sky backgrounds

## SNR and Session Planning

When planning a session (see [[Seasonal-Calendar]]), consider SNR impact:

1. **Prioritize altitude** — a target at 50deg gives ~30% more signal than the same target at 27deg
2. **Shoot during astronomical darkness** — even 2h of true dark sky beats 4h of nautical twilight
3. **Use the right filter** — [[Antlia-FQuad]] for emission nebulae, especially with any moon
4. **Plan multi-night campaigns** for challenging targets (low altitude or short dark windows)
5. **Be ruthless with rejection** — 30 excellent subs beat 50 mediocre ones
6. **Check existing integration** — diminishing returns above SNR 40; move to the next target

### Quick Decision Guide

```
Is the target an emission nebula?
├── YES → Use Quad Band
│   ├── Altitude > 40°? → 1-2 nights should suffice
│   ├── Altitude 25-40°? → Plan 3-5 nights minimum
│   └── No astro darkness? → Add 2-3x more integration
└── NO → Use L-Pro
    ├── Moon < 50%? → Proceed normally
    └── Moon > 50%? → Postpone or switch to emission target with QB
```
