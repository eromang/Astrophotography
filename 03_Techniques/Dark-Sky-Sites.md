## Practical Decision Tree

```
Need to image tonight?
├── Mains power needed / lazy → balcony (the default)
├── Need open horizon (high-Dec target) but quick → schwebach (9 min, ×1.16 SNR)
├── Short session <2 h, want dark site → wahl ⭐ (23 min, fastest dark site, ×1.44 SNR)
├── Long session ≥3 h, want maximum darkness → hoscheid ⭐ (33 min, ×1.6 SNR vs balcony)
├── First-ever portable trip, want zero scouting → burfelt (34 min, designated viewpoint — but Hoscheid is the same drive so retire after first scout)
└── Going for faintest targets (Sh2-240, etc.) → hoscheid (best LP reduction available within reach)
```

### Best Site by Use Case

For specific situations not covered by the decision tree above, here is the recommendation matrix derived from the verified site data:

| Use case | Best site | Why | Watch out for |
|---|---|---|---|
| 🏠 **Default — most sessions** | `balcony` | Zero drive, mains power, S-window covers most DSOs | Dec > +50° unreachable |
| ⏱️ **Short dark session <2 h** | `wahl` ⭐ | Fastest dark site (23 min), ×1.44 SNR vs balcony | Forest 10–15° E/S obstruction |
| 🌌 **Long dark session ≥3 h, faint targets** | `hoscheid` ⭐ | Darkest verified site (SQM 21.23), ×1.60 vs balcony | +10 min one-way vs Wahl |
| 🚗 **Quick high-Dec portable (any direction)** | `schwebach` | 9 min drive, open horizon, ×1.16 SNR | CR24 main road headlights N |
| 🆕 **First-ever portable trip / zero scouting** | `burfelt` | Designated viewpoint, signage, parking | Retire after Hoscheid scouted (same drive, darker sky) |
| ⬆️ **Open N horizon (polar align, NCP, comets)** | `wahl` ⭐ | Explicit "open N/NE", no N LP source. Cleanest N quadrant of any verified site. | Kinigshaff hamlet is W (not N) |
| ⬇️ **Low-S transit targets (M16/M17 ~26°)** | `wahl` | Clear S over open plateau | Burfelt has Esch-sur-Sûre town in S valley — avoid |
| 🌃 **Maximum darkness, drive time no object** | `hoscheid` | SQM 21.23, just shy of Bortle 3 | Bocksberg (+0.04 mag) not worth +16 min and verified terrain problems |
| 🎯 **Quick test session, away from streetlights** | `septfontaines` | 3 min drive, open 4 quadrants, ~balcony darkness (×1.05) | SNR gain is in the model noise floor — not for faint targets |
| 🔋 **Power-limited / Jackery <60%** | `balcony` or `schwebach` | Mains, or 9 min site to minimize Jackery use | Avoid 33–49 min drives with low charge |
| ❄️ **Cold/frost session, dew concerns** | `balcony` | Mains-powered dew heaters | At portable sites: [[COOWOO-Dew-Heater]] mandatory + Jackery hit |
| 🧪 **Test new rig / shakedown** | `balcony` | Easy iteration, no drive penalty for retries | For portable shakedown: `septfontaines` (3 min) > `schwebach` (9 min) |
| 📡 **High-Dec circumpolar targets at meridian transit** (M81/M82, Heart/Soul, IC1396) | `hoscheid` (long session) or `wahl` (short) | These targets transit S of zenith at 70–90° altitude — pure SNR matters more than horizon | The "northern" targets are actually overhead-S at peak |
| 🛰️ **Mosaic / wide-field full 360° framing** | `wahl` or `hoscheid` | Both have full 360° open access at meaningful darkness | Balcony's S-window blocks half the sky |
| 🏭 **Industrial-zone fly-by? Don't.** | (none) | — | `bissen-plateau` (steel mill) and `bissen-thilmanyshaff` (ArcelorMittal SE) are hard rejected. |

> [!tip] Reading the table
> "Best site" is the **first** choice for that case. The "Watch out for" column captures the failure mode if the use case doesn't perfectly match — use it as a sanity check before committing to the drive. For tradeoff decisions between rows (e.g., "should I go to Wahl or Hoscheid tonight?"), use the [[#Practical Decision Tree|Decision Tree]] above which reduces it to session-length.

### SNR multipliers vs balcony

For a given integration time, expected SNR gain on broadband targets (narrowband gain is smaller because Quad Band already rejects most LP):

| Site | Broadband SNR multiplier | Equiv. integration ratio |
|------|--------------------------|--------------------------|
| Schwebach | ×1.16 | 75% of balcony time for same SNR |
| Burfelt | ×1.37 | 53% of balcony time for same SNR |
| Wahl | ×1.44 | 48% of balcony time for same SNR |
| Hoscheid | ×1.60 | 39% of balcony time for same SNR |

So 4h at Hoscheid ≈ 10h at balcony for the same SNR — significant for faint reflection nebulae and galaxies.

## Key Findings from the Survey

1. **No Bortle 3 within Luxembourg.** The darkest verified site (Hoscheid 21.23) is just 0.07 mag below the Bortle 3 threshold (~21.30). True Bortle 3 requires cross-border travel.

2. **Original drive estimates were systematically conservative — except Wiltz.** All 13 non-zero Luxembourg drive times re-verified by car on 2026-04-07. **12 of 13 came in faster** than the original estimate (corrections of −2 to −13 min, average **−6.4 min**). Only **Wiltz Plateau** went the opposite direction: 40→**46 min** (+6 min), because the route forces transit through Wiltz town (urban traffic + lower speed limits) rather than following the autoroute that serves Hoscheid. The complete corrected table:

   | Site | Original | Verified | Δ |
   |------|---------:|---------:|---:|
   | Septfontaines | 5 | **3** | −2 |
   | Helperknapp ridge | 5 | **5** | 0 |
   | Quatre-Vents | 10 | **8** | −2 |
   | Schwebach | 12 | **9** | −3 |
   | Useldange | 12 | **10** | −2 |
   | Bissen Plateau | 15 | **11** | −4 |
   | Wahl | 30 | **23** | −7 |
   | Lac H-S | 35 | **29** | −6 |
   | Hoscheid | 45 | **33** | −12 |
   | Burfelt | 40 | **34** | −6 |
   | Hosingen Park | 55 | **42** | −13 |
   | **Wiltz** | **40** | **46** | **+6** ⚠️ |
   | Bocksberg | 60 | **49** | −11 |

   Practical implications:
   - **Wahl ↔ Hoscheid trade-off is ×1.11 SNR for +10 min one-way** — cheap for sessions ≥3 h, but Wahl wins on net imaging window for short sessions <2 h.
   - **Burfelt collapses as a distinct preset** — at 34 min it's now within 1 min of Hoscheid (33), but with worse SQM (21.06 vs 21.23). The original "easier infrastructure" justification only holds for the first 1–2 trips before Hoscheid is scouted.
   - **Wiltz is now the worst per-minute return in the survey** — 46 min drive for SQM 20.86 (worse than Schwebach at 9 min and SQM 20.81). Confirmed rejection.
   - Beyond Hoscheid, returns flatten sharply: Bocksberg (49 min) buys only +0.04 mag SQM (×1.02 SNR) for +16 min drive — wiped out by Bocksberg's verified terrain problems.

3. **Drive time is NOT a reliable proxy for sky quality.** Wiltz Plateau (40 min) is brighter than Schwebach (12 min) due to Wiltz town's local light pollution. Distance from villages matters more than absolute distance from Tuntange.

4. **Three quality tiers emerged from the data:**
   ```
   Best (21.05–21.23)     → Hoscheid, Bocksberg, Lac H-S, Hosingen, Wahl, Burfelt
   Good (20.76–20.86)     → Schwebach, Useldange, Wiltz, Chapelle Saint Donat
   Marginal (20.59–20.70) → Balcony, Septfontaines, Rue de Tuntange (Helperknapp), Bissen Thilmanyshaff
   Average (20.41–20.48)  → Bissen Plateau, Quatre-Vents
   ```

5. **Quatre-Vents was a misnomer.** Despite the "four winds" name suggesting an exposed elevated lookout, it's actually the worst site in the survey at Bortle 5. Removed from the preset list.

### Bortle interpretation reminder

- **SQM 21.30+** = Bortle 3 (start to make the trip worthwhile)
- **SQM 21.69+** = Bortle 2 (rare, would justify camping trips)
- **SQM 21.89+** = Bortle 1 (basically wilderness only)
- For reference: Hoscheid (the darkest Luxembourg site) is **SQM 21.23** — Bortle 4 top end, just shy of Bortle 3

### What to look for

- **Bortle 3** = SQM ~21.30–21.69 — noticeably darker than Hoscheid, Milky Way structure clearly visible
- **Bortle 2** = SQM ~21.69–21.89 — exceptional, only achievable at remote sites
- **Eifel National Park** is a designated dark sky region (officially recognized by IDA — International Dark Sky Park certification effort underway in some areas)
- **Belgian Ardennes** has known dark zones around Saint-Hubert and the Plateau des Tailles

### Practical considerations for cross-border trips

| Factor | Notes |
|--------|-------|
| **Drive time** | 50–90 min one-way → 100–180 min round trip |
| **Setup time** | +60 min (unfamiliar site) |
| **Total overhead** | 3–5 hours of "lost" imaging time |
| **Battery** | Jackery 500 may be insufficient (autumn/winter especially — see [[Jackery-Explorer-500]]) |
| **Border crossing** | Schengen — no formal checks but ID required |
| **Currency** | All Eurozone, no FX issues |
| **Camping option** | If multi-night, the trip overhead amortizes |
| **Weather decoupling** | Different microclimate from Luxembourg — clouds may differ |

### When is a cross-border trip worth it?

**Yes:**
- You want a faint reflection nebula or galaxy where ×1.6 SNR (Hoscheid) isn't enough
- You're combining it with a weekend trip / camping
- Bortle 3+ is genuinely required for the project

**No:**
- Casual portable sessions
- Quad Band narrowband targets (filter rejection makes Bortle 4 vs 3 mostly irrelevant)
- When weather looks unstable
- When the Jackery is partially discharged

## Verification Workflow (for new sites)

Before adding any candidate as a [[session-plan]] preset:

1. **Check sky quality** — `https://clearoutside.com/forecast/{lat}/{lon}` — note SQM, Bortle, artificial brightness
2. **Cross-check** — `https://www.lightpollutionmap.info/`
3. **Verify horizon** — Google Maps satellite view, look for tree/building obstructions
4. **Verify access** — public road, parking, no trespassing issues
5. **Scout in daytime** — drive there once during daylight, find the exact spot
6. **Get exact coordinates** — Google Maps long-press, copy coordinates
7. **Get exact altitude** — `https://api.open-elevation.com/api/v1/lookup?locations={lat},{lon}`
8. **Add as preset** — edit `06_Metadata/Agents/Claude/commands/sessions/session-plan.md`
9. **Test** — `/session-plan tonight --location <new-name>`

## Naming Conventions for Lookout Sites

When scouting, names like these often indicate open, panoramic spots worth checking:

| French / German naming pattern | Meaning |
|--------------------------------|---------|
| Belvédère, Vue, Panorama | Designated viewpoint with cleared sightlines |
| Quatre-Vents, Vier-Winde, Hochstrooss | "Four winds" / high road = exposed plateau |
| -bierg, -berg | Hill / mountain — usually open at the top |
| -platenge, -plateau | Plateau — open horizon by definition |
| Bocksberg, Stuesselbierg, Schoumansbierg | Known Luxembourg lookout hills |
| Tour, Turm | Observation tower — guaranteed clear horizon |

> **Caveat:** Quatre-Vents (verified Bortle 5) shows that names alone don't guarantee dark skies — verify before driving.

## Constraints & Tradeoffs

| Constraint | Impact | Mitigation |
|-----------|--------|------------|
| **Drive time** | Eats imaging window, requires early departure | Pick wahl for routine, hoscheid only for projects |
| **Setup time** | ~45 min for unfamiliar site | Scout in daytime first; standardize the rig packing |
| **Power (Jackery 500)** | Limited summer/winter (see [[Jackery-Explorer-500#Verdict by Season]]) | Solar panel for multi-night, or shorter sessions |
| **Weather** | Forecast may differ from balcony | Re-check forecast for actual coordinates before driving |
| **Access at night** | Some sites have gated parking or no road lighting | Verify access during scouting; bring red headlamp |
| **Personal safety** | Remote sites alone at night | Tell someone where you're going; verify phone signal |
| **Dew & frost** | Open plateaus collect more dew than balcony | [[COOWOO-Dew-Heater]] essential, especially autumn/winter |
